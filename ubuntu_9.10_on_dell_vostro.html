Installing Ubuntu 9.10 (Karmic Koala) on Dell Vostro A860

Last updated: 2010-04-24

General Hardware Specifications of Dell Vostro A860:

Hardware Components
Status under Linux
Notes
Intel® GM965 Express chipset Works No special procedure required during installation.
Mobile Intel® Core™ 2 Duo T5870, 2000 MHz Works No special procedure required during installation.
15.6" WXGA HD Display (1366x768) Works No special procedure required during installation.
Intel Graphics x3100 Works Haven't tried 3D games...
2048MB DDR2/800MHz RAM Works No special procedure required during installation
250GB 5400RPM SATA Hard Drive Works No special procedure required during installation
8x DVD+-RW DL drive Works No special procedure required during installation
Integrated Sound Card Works No special procedure required during installation
10/100mbps LAN Integrated Network Card Works No special procedure required during installation
Wireless 802.11b/g Works The Network Manager of Kubuntu was unable to connect to my WPA encrypted wireless network. Ubuntu had no such problem.
5in1 Card reader Works Tried only with SD Card
Bluetooth interface Doesn't work due to this bug The Bluetooth and the phone find each other, but the data transfer does not work.
FireWire I-link Haven't tried
PCMCIA (PC Card) Haven't tried
USB 2.0 (2 slots) Works No special procedure required during installation. The laptop can boot from a USB flash drive.
External VGA interface Works External monitor can be plugged in when the OS is already running. "System > Preferences > Display" works fine. Both the external monitor and the laptop's own screen can run at the same time. If you want the external screen to be the left one, first turn off the laptop's own screen (from "System > Preferences > Display"), then turn it on again.
4200 mAh Lithium-Ion Battery Works No special procedure required during installation. The battery life is around 3 and a half hours.

This laptop is operating under Kernel version 2.6.31. I tried both the 32bit and the 64bit versions; there is not much difference.

Unresolved issues
  • Hibernate does not work. Suspend to RAM works. Another Dell Vostro A860 review writes that "After hibernating, I was unable to reconnect to a network until rebooting. Again, might be a coincidence". I had the same issue when I used the "hibernate" command.
  • Adobe Flash usually works (rarely Youtube videos have to be reloaded).
  • If I close the lid, the screen goes turns off for a second, then it turns on again, so it is unnecessarily on.
